Mahatma Gandhi

Introduced us to Nonviolent resistance.

Time line of Gandhi's life:

  • 2 October 1869 Born in Porbandar, Porbandar State, Kathiawar Agency, British Raj (India)
  • 1893 Moved to South Africa to represent an Indian merchant in a lawsuit.
  • 1914 Mahatma (honorable person) First applied ito him .
  • 1915Aged 45, he returned to India and soon set about organising peasants, farmers, and urban labourers to protest against excessive land-tax and discrimination.
  • 1921 Gandhi led nationwide campaigns for easing poverty, expanding women's rights, building religious and ethnic amity, ending untouchability, and, above all, achieving swaraj or self-rule.
  • 1930 Bringing anti-colonial nationalism to the common Indians, Gandhi led them in challenging the British-imposed salt tax with the 400 km (250 mi) Dandi Salt March
  • 1942 He started Quit India Movement.
  • 1948 Shot dead by firing three bullets into his chest at an interfaith prayer meeting in Delhi
  • 2 October is commemorated in India as Gandhi Jayanti, a national holiday, and worldwide as the International Day of Nonviolence.
